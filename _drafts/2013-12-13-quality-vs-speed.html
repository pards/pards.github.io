I refer to this piece posted by James Shore.

What matters more, code quality or quick delivery?

I agree with James that what matters depends on who you ask. I have seen many software projects achieve great things in very short periods of time and hailed as huge success stories only to get re-written a few years later.

The investment banking and capital markets environments are notorious for this. Many banks claim that time-to-market is their competitive advantage and that they can't afford to invest in things such as automated tests and deployments.

But if the software is so important, then can you really afford to throw it all away in a few years' time to re-write it, all the while losing market share to your competitors?
